SLE 2017 paper:
Better call the crowd: using crowdsourcing to shape the notation of domain-specific languages

Marco Brambilla, Jordi Cabot, Javier Luis Cánovas Izquierdo, Andrea Mauri

  • DOI: 10.1145/3136014.3136033
  • T5H: Usability
    • The paper’s main aim is improving usability/acceptance of DSML notations by involving end-users via crowdsourcing.
  • T1A: Design
    • It directly concerns the design of a language’s notation (concrete syntax) informed by user feedback.
  • T5E: Empirical Evaluation
    • Crowdsourcing implies empirical collection and analysis of user preferences over notational alternatives.
  • T3C: DSLs
    • The setting is domain-specific modeling languages whose notations are being designed.
