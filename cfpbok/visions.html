Vision / new ideas papers

Visionary papers, also known as new ideas papers, are sought after in some CfPs as shorter form papers with provocative content which has not enjoyed full evaluation.

  • (2017) Red Shift: Procedural Shift-Reduce Parsing by Nicolas Laurent
  • (2018) Input-Driven Regular Expressions by Alexander Sakharov
  • (2018) Languages as First-Class Citizens by Matteo Cimini
  • (2018) Shape-diverse DSLs: Languages without Borders by Fabien Coulon, Thomas Degueule, Tijs van der Storm, Benoît Combemale
  • (2019) A Vision of Miking: Interactive Programmatic Modeling, Sound Language Composition, and Self-Learning Compilation by David Broman
  • (2019) Breaking Parsers: Mutation-Based Generation of Programs with Guaranteed Syntax Errors by Moeketsi Raselimo, Jan Taljaard, Bernd Fischer
  • (2019) Generating Incremental Type Services by André Pacak, Sebastian Erdweg
  • (2021) The Next 700 Language Workbenches by Mikhail Barash
  • (2021) Automated Engineering of Metamorphic Testing Environments for Domain-Specific Languages by Pablo C. Cañizares, Pablo Gómez-Abajo, Alberto Núñez, Esther Guerra, Juan de Lara
  • (2021) Bias in Systematic Grammar-based Test Suite Construction Algorithms by Christoff Rossouw, Bernd Fischer
  • (2022) Reflection as a Tool to Debug Objects by Steven Costiou, Vincent Aranega, Marcus Denker
  • (2022) Neural Language Models and Few Shot Learning for Systematic Requirements Processing in MDSE by Vincent Bertram, Miriam Boß, Evgeny Kusmenko, Imke Helene Nachmann, Bernhard Rumpe, Danilo Trotta, Louis Wachtmeister
  • (2023) Seamless Code Generator Synchronization in the Composition of Heterogeneous Modeling Languages by Nico Jansen, Bernhard Rumpe
  • (2023) Online Name-Based Navigation for Software Meta-languages by Peter D. Mosses
  • (2024) Method Bundles by Dimi Racordon, Dave Abrahams
  • (2025) Integrating Model Checking into a Live Modeling Environment by Joeri Exelmans, Ciprian Teodorov, Hans Vangheluwe
