ATEM 2006 SI paper:
Extending Grammars and Metamodels for Reuse: the Reuseware Approach

Jakob Henriksson, Florian Heidenreich, Jendrik Johannes, Steffen Zschaler, Uwe Aßmann

  • DOI: 10.1049/iet-sen:20070060
  • Extended version of How Dark Should a Component Black-Box Be? The Reuseware Answer (WCOP 2007) & Reuseware – Adding Modularity to Your Language of Choice (TOOLS 2007)
  • T1E: Reuse
    • The paper proposes language-independent mechanisms to enable reusable, composable partial programs as a systematic reuse feature for DSLs.
  • T1D: Composition
    • It introduces composition constructs and a composition process to assemble encapsulated language components into complete programs.
  • T4A: Workbenches
    • It presents the Reuseware Composition Framework as tool support for implementing the proposed reuse/composition approach.
  • T3A: Meta-languages
    • It operates on grammars and metamodels as language definition artefacts that are extended to support reuse.
