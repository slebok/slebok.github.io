SLE 2015 paper:
An empirical study on simplification of business process modeling languages

Eric Umuhoza, Marco Brambilla, Davide Ripamonti, Jordi Cabot

  • DOI: 10.1145/2814251.2814254
  • T5E: Empirical Evaluation
    • The paper’s primary contribution is an empirical study evaluating language simplification effects.
  • T1A: Design
    • It concerns redesign/simplification choices in the design of a modeling language.
  • T2B: Evolution
    • Simplification is framed as adapting and maintaining a language for practical use over time.
  • T3C: DSLs
    • Business process modeling languages function as domain-specific languages for process modeling.
